Chapter 01 · The Source

From the volcanic slopes,
to the cup in your hand.

Rooted in the highlands of Mbinga, Mbozi, and Karagwe. A Tanzanian house, returning African coffee to its rightful place.

Chapter 02 · The Harvest

The Chioda Rhythm.

Hand-picked at peak ripeness. Smallholder cooperatives in Mbinga harvest only the deep red cherries. Traceable, ethical, and honored at every step.

Chapter 03 · Selection

Only the ripest survive.

Sorted by sight, by touch, by patience.

Chapter 04 · The Mill

Sun-dried & Sorted.

At the Kachebonaho mills, parchment is slow-dried on raised beds — preserving the unique floral and citrus notes of the highland soil.

Chapter 05 · The Roastery

The Ceremony of Roast.

Roasted in small batches in Dar es Salaam. Profiles named for the rituals they honor — Chioda for harvest, Ingoma for the warrior.

Chapter 06 · Precision
